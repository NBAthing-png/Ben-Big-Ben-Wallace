Ben Wallace

"No Meat, No Drink, No Damn Body."

"Heart over height. Undrafted, unheralded, but never outworked."

The Motown Anchor

Ben Wallace is widely regarded as one of the greatest defensive players in NBA history. Going undrafted out of Virginia Union University in 1996, Wallace fought his way into the league through sheer grit, rebounding tenacity, and elite rim protection.


Standing at a listed 6'9" (though often rumored to be closer to 6'7"), "Big Ben" routinely shut down the league's most dominant 7-foot centers. As the defensive anchor of the iconic 2003-2004 Detroit Pistons "Goin' to Work" era, he led the team to a legendary upset championship victory over the heavily favored Los Angeles Lakers.
